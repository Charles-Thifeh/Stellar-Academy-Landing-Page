Our Approach

How We Teach

Inquiry-Based Learning

Scholars are taught to ask questions, investigate problems, and draw conclusions — building genuine understanding rather than rote memorisation.

Collaborative Classrooms

Small class sizes and group-based projects develop communication, teamwork, and leadership skills alongside academic knowledge.

Technology Integration

Our modern computer lab and in-class digital resources ensure scholars are confident, responsible, and skilled digital citizens.

Continuous Assessment

Regular formative assessments, termly reports, and parent conferences ensure every scholar's progress is tracked, celebrated, and supported.

Differentiated Instruction

Teachers are trained to adapt their methods to diverse learning styles and abilities, ensuring no scholar is left behind or unchallenged.

Excellence & Recognition

From termly honour rolls to annual awards, we celebrate academic achievement and positive character as equally worthy of recognition.

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Can't find your answer? Contact us directly.

SSA follows the Nigerian National Curriculum as prescribed by the Federal Ministry of Education, enriched with our own pastoral programme, co-curricular activities, and character development modules. At the senior secondary level, scholars are prepared for WAEC and NECO external examinations.

We deliberately keep our class sizes small to maximise individual attention. Pre-school classes hold a maximum of 15 pupils, Grade classes up to 25, and secondary classes between 25 and 30. This ensures every scholar receives the focus they deserve.

Comprehensive academic reports are issued at the end of every term alongside a parent-teacher conference opportunity. Continuous assessment scores are shared throughout the term, and parents can request progress meetings at any time through the school office.

Absolutely. We offer a rich programme of clubs and activities including debating, chess, drama, music, visual arts, football, athletics, coding club, and more. Extra-curricular participation is encouraged as part of our commitment to holistic development.

The school gate opens at 7:00am. Formal lessons begin at 8:00am. Grade school closes at 2:00pm, while secondary school closes at 3:30pm. An after-school care programme is available until 5:30pm for an additional fee.

Yes. SSA scholars wear a distinctive white and maroon uniform that reflects our school colours and values of purity and purpose. Full uniform details, including sportswear, are available in our prospectus and from the school office upon enrolment.

Applications can be made online via our Admissions page or by visiting the school office in person. You will need to complete the application form, submit the required documents, and attend an informal assessment session. We recommend applying early as places fill quickly.
